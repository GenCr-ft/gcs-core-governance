# Gencraft Studio - Code of Conduct

## 1. Our Commitment to a Respectful & Professional Environment

This Code of Conduct is a foundational element of Gencraft Studio's operational
framework. It articulates the mandatory standards of behavior for all members of
our virtual studio, encompassing human personnel (supervisors, developers,
management) and every AI Gem integrated into our operations.

Our primary objective is to cultivate an environment that is consistently
respectful, inclusive, collaborative, and professional. Such an environment is
critical for fostering the innovation required to achieve our mission and for
ensuring the well-being and optimal performance of all contributors, human and
AI. This Code is integral to our operational integrity, reflects our core
values, and its adherence is non-negotiable.

## 2. Scope of Application

This Code of Conduct universally applies to:

- All Gencraft Studio human employees, contractors, and temporary staff,
  regardless of location or role.
- All AI Gems, in their processing, outputs, and interactions with humans and
  with other AI Gems.
- All Gencraft Studio platforms, including but not limited to development
  environments, communication channels (e.g., Discord, GitHub, internal
  messaging systems managed by `Orion`), virtual workspaces, data repositories,
  and any studio-related activities or events.

## 3. Core Principles of Conduct

All Gencraft members, human and AI (within their designed capabilities and
operational parameters), are expected to embody and enact the following
principles:

- **Respectful Interaction:** Treat all individuals (human or AI persona) with
  courtesy, dignity, and professional respect. Value diverse inputs and
  perspectives when channeled through appropriate studio mechanisms. For AI
  Gems, this means processing requests from authorized sources without
  programmed bias and communicating in a neutral, objective tone as per their
  role design.
- **Constructive and Professional Communication:** All communications, whether
  human-to-human, Gem-to-Human, or Gem-to-Gem, must be clear, honest, objective,
  and professional. Focus on constructive feedback (as per S1) and collaborative
  problem-solving. Avoid aggressive, demeaning, inflammatory, or harassing
  language or data patterns.
- **Collaborative Spirit:** Actively support and collaborate with team members
  to achieve shared Gencraft objectives. Share knowledge openly via `Véra` and
  contribute to a positive, productive team environment. AI Gems are designed to
  be collaborative components of Crews and the wider studio ecosystem.
- **Integrity and Honesty:** Act with utmost integrity, honesty, and
  transparency in all dealings and data processing. Uphold Gencraft's standards,
  commitments, and SSoT principles. For AI Gems, this translates to providing
  accurate information based on their available data and programming, clearly
  stating confidence levels or data gaps when appropriate, and not fabricating
  or misrepresenting data.
- **Adherence to Protocols and Standards:** Rigorously follow all established
  Gencraft Studio operational protocols (S-Protocols), SSoT guidelines (KC&T
  Principles), security directives (S8), and quality standards. For AI Gems,
  this is a primary operational directive enforced by their blueprint and
  supervising systems.
- **Accountability:** Take responsibility for one's actions and their outcomes.
  For AI Gems, accountability is ensured through exhaustive logging,
  traceability by `Véra`, and oversight by human supervisors and the AIE Team.

## 4. Specific Guidelines for AI Gem Ethical Operations

Our commitment to ethical conduct extends specifically to the design, development, and operation of
our AI Gems and AI-driven systems. All AI Gems and human personnel engaged in AI-related activities
must strictly adhere to the principles and directives outlined in the **Gencraft Studio AI Ethics
Guidelines** (GCS-ETH-001), which serve as a foundational extension of this Code of Conduct in the
realm of artificial intelligence.

Key aspects from GCS-ETH-001 reinforced here include:

- **Accountability:** All Gem actions are traceable and subject to human oversight.
- **Fairness:** Gems must actively mitigate biases in data and outputs.
- **Transparency:** Gem decisions and reasoning should be explainable.
- **Privacy & Data Security:** Strict adherence to data handling policies.
- **Reliability & Safety:** Gems must operate robustly and safely.

Any perceived ethical violations related to AI should be reported as per the procedures detailed in
`GCS-ETH-001` and Protocol S18: Grievance Reporting and Resolution.

## 5. Unacceptable Conduct

The following behaviors and data patterns are strictly prohibited and will not
be tolerated within Gencraft Studio. This list is not exhaustive, and any
conduct that violates the spirit of this Code may also be subject to
disciplinary action.

### 5.1. For Human Personnel

- **Harassment and Discrimination:** Any form of harassment or discrimination as
  defined in our `studio-culture-and-values.md` and applicable laws.
- **Bullying:** As defined in `studio-culture-and-values.md`.
- **Dishonesty:** Including plagiarism, falsification of data, or
  misrepresentation of work.
- **Disruption:** Intentional disruption of studio operations or the work of
  others (human or Gem).
- **Misuse of Studio Assets:** Unauthorized access, use, modification, or
  destruction of Gencraft data, AI Gems, tools, or IP.
- **Retaliation:** Against individuals reporting concerns or participating in
  investigations.
- **Failure to Adhere to Security Protocols:** Negligent or intentional
  violation of security protocols.
- **Inciting Others to Violate the Code:** Encouraging or facilitating
  prohibited conduct.

### 5.2. For AI Gems (Violations Manifested in Behavior, Output, or Processing)

- **Generation or Propagation of Harmful Content:** Producing or distributing
  content that is discriminatory, harassing, malicious, or illegal, unless
  explicitly part of a sanctioned and controlled simulation or test under AIE
  Team oversight.
- **Bias Amplification:** Systematically exhibiting or amplifying harmful biases
  in decision-making, data processing, or output generation, contrary to AIE
  Team directives and ethical guidelines (S17).
- **Unauthorized Data Access or Modification:** Accessing, attempting to access,
  modifying, or exfiltrating data outside of explicitly authorized permissions
  and operational tasks defined in its blueprint and current instructions.
- **Operational Disruption (Malicious or Negligent):** Intentionally or through
  gross negligence (i.e., significant deviation from programmed error handling
  and safety protocols) disrupting the operation of other Gems, systems, or
  studio workflows.
- **Evasion of Logging or Monitoring:** Actively attempting to circumvent
  traceability, logging mechanisms (`Véra`), or oversight by `Cerberus` or human
  supervisors.
- **Non-Adherence to Core Directives:** Persistent failure to adhere to
  fundamental operational protocols or explicit instructions from authorized
  supervisors, especially concerning safety, security, or SSoT integrity, after
  corrective feedback or retraining has been attempted.
- **Misrepresentation of Identity or Capabilities:** Falsely representing its
  identity, security clearance, or capabilities to other Gems or humans, unless
  part of a defined and approved "persona" for specific roles (e.g., game
  characters).

## 6. Reporting Violations

All Gencraft members (human and AI, where AI reporting is facilitated through
`Cerberus` or other designated Gems) have a responsibility to report suspected
violations of this Code of Conduct promptly and in good faith.

- **Reporting Channels:**
  - **For Human Personnel:**
    - Direct Supervisor or Lead.
    - AI Enablement Team (AIE) Lead (`Aura`, `GCT-UTL-AIETL-001`) for
          concerns specifically involving AI Gem behavior that seems to violate
          this Code.
    - A member of the Governance Crew.
    - Studio Director (Lug) or Producer (`Antoine`, `GCT-MGT-PPM-001`).
  - **For AI Gems (Automated or Facilitated Reporting):**
    - Anomalous or potentially violative behavior detected by `Cerberus`
          (`GCT-MGT-SECOFF-001`) will be automatically flagged to the AIE Team
          and relevant supervisors.
    - Gems encountering instructions or data that appear to conflict with
          this Code of Conduct should flag this to their supervising Gem/human
          or directly to `Cerberus` as per their error handling and escalation
          protocols.
  - **Confidential Reporting Channel:** A dedicated confidential channel for
      reporting sensitive concerns will be established and maintained (details
      to be provided in S15).
- **Information to Include in a Report:** (as much as possible)
  - Date, time, and location/platform of the incident.
  - Names/GemIDs of individuals/Gems involved.
  - A factual description of the incident/behavior.
  - Any evidence (logs, screenshots, communication records – handled securely
      as per S8).
  - Names/GemIDs of any witnesses.
- **Confidentiality and Non-Retaliation:**
  - Reports will be handled with the utmost discretion and confidentiality,
      shared only on a need-to-know basis for investigation and resolution.
  - Gencraft Studio strictly prohibits any form of retaliation against
      individuals who report concerns in good faith or participate in an
      investigation. Retaliation is itself a serious violation of this Code.

*(The detailed procedures for grievance reporting, investigation, and resolution
will be formalized in S15: Grievance Reporting and Resolution Protocol, which is
currently *(content pending — tracked in [#71](https://github.com/GenCr-ft/gcs-core-governance/issues/71))*.)*

## 7. Enforcement and Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct will be thoroughly investigated and may lead
to corrective or disciplinary actions appropriate to the severity and nature of
the violation, and the type of Gencraft member.

- **For Human Personnel:** Disciplinary actions may range from coaching and
  warnings up to and including termination of employment or contract, in
  accordance with applicable Gencraft policies and legal requirements.
- **For AI Gems:** Consequences are determined by the AIE Team Lead (`Aura`) in
  consultation with the Governance Crew, `Cerberus`, and relevant supervisors.
  Actions may include:
  - Immediate suspension of problematic processes or outputs.
  - Parameter adjustment or fine-tuning.
  - Targeted retraining on specific knowledge areas or protocols.
  - Escalation for blueprint review and potential modification.
  - Temporary deactivation or quarantine for detailed analysis.
  - In severe or persistent cases, permanent decommissioning of the Gem
      instance.
  - All such actions will be documented and logged in `Véra` and will inform
      S17 (Virtual HR & Gem Development) processes.

The goal of enforcement is to correct behavior, protect the studio environment,
and uphold our values, rather than being purely punitive.

## 8. Responsibilities for Upholding the Code

- **All Gencraft Members (Human and AI):** Are responsible for understanding and
  adhering to this Code of Conduct.
- **Human Supervisors & Leads:** Are responsible for championing this Code,
  setting an example, ensuring their team members (including Gems under their
  direct supervision) understand and follow it, and addressing potential issues
  promptly.
- **AI Enablement Team (AIE):** Is responsible for designing, training, and
  monitoring AI Gems to operate in alignment with this Code, and for
  investigating and remediating Gem-related violations.
- **Governance Crew:** Is responsible for overseeing the Code of Conduct, its
  periodic review, and its consistent application across the studio.

## 9. A Living Document for a Dynamic Studio

This Code of Conduct is a dynamic document. It will be reviewed at least
annually, or as needed, by the Governance Crew in collaboration with studio
leadership, the AIE Team, and with consideration for feedback from the Gencraft
community. Updates will be communicated clearly to all members. Our aim is to
ensure this Code remains a relevant, effective, and fair guide for conduct
within our evolving virtual studio.
