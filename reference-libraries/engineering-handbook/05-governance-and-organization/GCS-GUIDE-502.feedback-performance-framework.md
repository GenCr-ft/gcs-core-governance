# The Feedback & Performance Review Framework

## 1. Objective

This guide establishes the official studio framework for giving and receiving feedback and for conducting formal performance reviews. Its purpose is to foster a culture of continuous improvement, transparency, and fairness, where every team member has a clear understanding of their performance and a path for growth.

---

## 2. Our Feedback Philosophy

* **Feedback is a Gift**: We treat feedback as a valuable gift intended to help individuals and the team grow. It is delivered with positive intent and received with an open mind.
* **Continuous and Timely**: Feedback should not be saved for the annual review. It should be a continuous, real-time process. Timely feedback is more effective.
* **Specific and Actionable**: Vague feedback is not helpful. All feedback should be specific, based on observable behaviors or data, and should suggest actionable steps for improvement.

---

## 3. The Feedback Protocol

### 3.1. One-on-One (1:1) Meetings

* **Rhythm**: The 1:1 meeting between an engineer and their manager is the primary forum for continuous feedback. These meetings **MUST** occur at least bi-weekly.
* **Structure**: This is the engineer's meeting, not the manager's. The engineer should drive the agenda, discussing progress, challenges, career goals, and any other topics. The manager's role is to listen, ask questions, and provide coaching and support.

### 3.2. Giving Constructive Feedback

When delivering constructive feedback, the **Situation-Behavior-Impact (SBI)** model is our standard protocol:

* **Situation**: Describe the specific situation where the behavior occurred (e.g., "During yesterday's planning meeting...").
* **Behavior**: Describe the specific, observable behavior. Avoid generalizations or interpretations (e.g., "...you interrupted the designer twice while they were presenting," NOT "...you were being disrespectful.").
* **Impact**: Explain the impact of the behavior on you, the team, or the project (e.g., "The impact was that we lost the thread of the design discussion, and it made the designer hesitant to share more ideas.").

---

## 4. The Performance Review Process

Performance reviews are a formal process that occurs on a semi-annual basis. They are a look-back at past performance and a look-forward to future growth.

### 4.1. The Review Cycle

* **Self-Review**: The engineer completes a self-review, reflecting on their accomplishments, challenges, and growth against the competencies defined in our Career Path Guide.
* **Peer Feedback**: The engineer's manager collects written feedback from a selection of the engineer's peers (typically 3-5). This feedback is structured around the four competency areas.
* **Manager Review**: The manager synthesizes the self-review, peer feedback, and their own observations into a comprehensive performance summary.
* **Calibration**: Managers meet to calibrate their reviews, ensuring that standards are applied consistently and fairly across the entire engineering organization.
* **Delivery**: The manager delivers the review to the engineer in a dedicated meeting.

### 4.2. Outputs of the Review

The performance review process has three primary outputs:

* **Performance Summary**: A clear assessment of the engineer's performance against the expectations for their level.
* **Development Goals**: A set of 2-3 specific, actionable goals for the next six months, co-created by the engineer and their manager.
* **Promotion & Compensation Decisions**: The review cycle is the primary input for decisions regarding promotions and compensation adjustments.
