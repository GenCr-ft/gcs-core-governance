# The Engineering Career Path & Competency Matrix

## 1. Objective

This guide defines the official engineering career paths and competency expectations at the studio. Its purpose is to provide a clear, transparent, and consistent framework for career development, performance evaluation, and promotions. It ensures that every member of the engineering team understands what is expected of them at their current level and what is required to advance.

## 2. Our Career Philosophy

We support two distinct and equally valuable career paths for senior engineers:

* **Individual Contributor (IC) Path:** This path is for engineers who want to grow by deepening their technical expertise and solving increasingly complex technical problems. Their influence grows through technical leadership, mentorship, and innovation.
* **Manager Path:** This path is for engineers who want to grow by focusing on people management, team building, and organizational leadership. Their influence grows by empowering and scaling their teams.

Switching between paths is possible and supported, in discussion with an engineer's manager.

## 3. Engineering Levels & Competency Areas

Our career ladder is defined by a series of levels. For each level, expectations are defined across four key competency areas.

| Level | Title | Path | Summary |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ENG 1 | Junior Engineer | IC | Learns core skills, requires significant guidance. |
| ENG 2 | Engineer | IC | Works independently on well-defined tasks. |
| ENG 3 | Senior Engineer | IC | Owns complex features, mentors others, influences team-level decisions. |
| ENG 4 | Staff Engineer | IC | Solves ambiguous, organization-wide technical problems. |
| MGR 1 | Engineering Manager | Manager | Manages a single team, focuses on execution and people growth. |
| MGR 2 | Senior Engineering Manager | Manager | Manages multiple teams or a critical, large-scale area. |

### 3.1. Competency Area 1: Technical Skill & Execution

* **Description:** The ability to design, build, and deliver high-quality, reliable software.
* **Examples at different levels:**
  * **ENG 1:** Writes clean, tested code for small, well-scoped tasks.
  * **ENG 3:** Designs and implements multi-component systems, handles technical debt.
  * **ENG 4:** Architects solutions to large-scale, ambiguous technical challenges that affect multiple teams.

### 3.2. Competency Area 2: Collaboration & Communication

* **Description:** The ability to work effectively with others and communicate ideas clearly.
* **Examples at different levels:**
  * **ENG 1:** Asks clarifying questions, provides constructive feedback in code reviews.
  * **ENG 3:** Mentors junior engineers, communicates technical decisions clearly to non-technical stakeholders.
  * **MGR 1:** Facilitates team discussions, manages conflicts, communicates team status upwards.

### 3.3. Competency Area 3: Influence & Leadership

* **Description:** The ability to have a positive impact beyond one's immediate tasks.
* **Examples at different levels:**
  * **ENG 2:** Improves team documentation and processes.
  * **ENG 3:** Leads the technical design for a major feature, drives adoption of a new best practice.
  * **ENG 4:** Sets the technical direction for a whole domain, represents the studio at industry conferences.

### 3.4. Competency Area 4: Business Acumen & Customer Focus

* **Description:** The ability to understand and align one's work with business goals and customer needs.
* **Examples at different levels:**
  * **ENG 1:** Understands the user value of the story they are working on.
  * **ENG 3:** Challenges requirements that don't align with user needs or business goals, suggests technical alternatives.
  * **MGR 2:** Works with product leadership to define the technical roadmap for a business area.

## 4. Promotion Process

Promotions are based on a demonstrated and sustained impact at the *next* level, not just excellence at the current level. The process is initiated by an engineer's manager during the regular performance review cycle [cite: GCS-GUIDE-502] and requires a formal promotion packet with evidence and feedback from peers.

## 5. Career Development Resources

We provide various resources to help engineers grow in their careers:

* **Mentorship Program:** Pairing with senior engineers for guidance and support.
* **Training & Workshops:** Regular technical and soft skills training sessions.
* **Career Development Plans:** Individualized plans created in collaboration with managers to set goals and track progress.
* **Feedback Culture:** Regular feedback sessions to help engineers understand their strengths and areas for improvement.
